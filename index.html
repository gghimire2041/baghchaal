Bagh-Chal: The Ancient Game of Tigers and Goats

Discover the timeless strategy of Nepal! Bagh-chal (Nepali: ‡§¨‡§æ‡§ò ‡§ö‡§æ‡§≤, meaning "tiger game") is a strategic, two-player board game that originated in Nepal. This ancient game represents the eternal struggle between predator and prey, where one player controls four tigers and the other player controls up to twenty goats. The tigers 'hunt' the goats while the goats attempt to block the tigers' movements.

"Originally, people would carve the board's 5x5 grid into sand or stone, and use pebbles as game pieces. The theme of the game mimicked the threat of tigers on the hunt for animals that strayed from the herd." - This 1000-year-old game reflects the deep connection between Nepali culture and the natural world.

The game has survived till now from one generation to another through imitation and oral traditions. But the game still might be saved also because of technology. There are online versions of the game as well as tablet/mobile game apps. Today, you can experience this cultural treasure digitally, helping preserve this ancient wisdom for future generations.

Game Rules

üéØ Objective

  • Tigers: Capture five goats to win
  • Goats: Block all tigers so they cannot move

üéÆ How to Play

  • At the start of the game all four tigers are placed on the four corners of the grid, facing the center. No goats are placed on the board during the initial setup.
  • Phase 1: If the goat player has goats in hand, he must use his turn to place one on any empty point on the board.
  • Phase 2: Once all of the goats have been placed on the board, goats must move in the same fashion as the tigers, one intersection to another.

  • To eat a goat, the tiger player must jump with one of his tigers along a marked line, over an adjacent goat, to an empty point beyond.
  • A tiger cannot jump over another tiger.
  • They can capture only one goat at a time.

üèÜ Winning

  • A game is won by the tigers if they capture (eat) 5 goats.
  • A game is won by the goats if they succeed in preventing any tiger from being able to move.
